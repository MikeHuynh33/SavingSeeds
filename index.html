 Helpful tips:

S&P500

Historically, the SQ500 has had an average return of around 10% per year.Investing in the SQ500 can be a good option for those who want to participate in the potential growth of the US economy as a whole, rather than trying to pick individual stocks or invest in specific sectors.

Nasdaq

Over the long-term, the NASDAQ Composite has historically delivered strong returns, with an average annual return of around 9% over the past 10 years.Investing in the NASDAQ can provide exposure to a diverse range of technology companies, which may have the potential for higher growth compared to more established companies in other sectors

Bonds

In general, bonds are considered a lower risk investment compared to stocks, and they typically offer a lower rate of return. The average annual return for bonds over the past 10 years (as of 2021) has been around 3-4%, depending on the type of bond and the specific time period being considered.

Dividend

In general, companies that pay stock dividends typically do so at a rate of 2-4% of the stock's price per year. However, this rate can vary widely based on a number of factors, including the company's financial health, growth prospects, and dividend policy.
